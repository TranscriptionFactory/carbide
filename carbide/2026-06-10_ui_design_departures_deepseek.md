# Dramatic UI Design Departures for Carbide

_Generated via deepseek — 2026-06-10_

Ten radical alternatives to the current VS Code-inspired IDE layout, ranging from spatial canvases to ambient HUDs.

---

## 1. Infinite Canvas / Spatial-First

The canvas *is* the workspace. Notes are cards arranged freely in 2D space. Pan, zoom, cluster. Connections are visible lines. The file tree, tabs, and sidebar dissolve — you navigate spatially. Think Kinopio meets Figma.

## 2. Conversational / AI-First

A persistent chat thread is the primary interface. Notes are artifacts the AI helps you create, organize, and retrieve. You ask "what was that idea about X?" instead of navigating a file tree. The editor becomes secondary.

## 3. Outliner-First (Workflowy/Roam style)

A single infinite outline replaces files, folders, and tabs. Every line is addressable, nestable, and linkable. Navigation is expanding/collapsing branches. No separate "file" concept — just a tree of thoughts.

## 4. Zettelkasten Card Grid

Every note is an atomic card in a massive, filterable, taggable waterfall/grid. Search and filter, not folders, drive navigation. Think Are.na or a hyper-polished Pinterest for thoughts.

## 5. Timeline / Journal-First

Notes are organized chronologically by default (like a journal/feed). Calendar heatmaps, daily notes as the hub, scroll-back-through-time as primary navigation. Files and folders are secondary views.

## 6. Ambient / Context-Aware HUD

The app fades into the background. It surfaces relevant notes based on what you're doing (active window, time of day, recent activity). A minimal floating overlay or desktop widget rather than a full workspace. Calm technology approach.

## 7. Mind Map / Radial

Everything radiates from a central node. You traverse your vault by expanding and contracting branches radially. No linear file tree — just concentric rings of connected ideas.

## 8. Dual-Mode: Capture vs. Refine

Two completely separate UIs. **Capture mode**: always-available minimal scratchpad (menu bar app, global hotkey, zero chrome). **Refine mode**: a focused, immersive editor — but drastically simpler than the current workspace (no activity bar, no bottom panel, no context rail by default).

## 9. Block-Referencing Network (Roam-like)

Every paragraph/block is a first-class entity with its own ID. Transclusion, block references, and bidirectional links are the core interaction model. The UI centers on block-level queries and embedded references rather than "opening files."

## 10. Physical Notebook Metaphor

Full skeuomorphic design — page-turning animations, physical tab dividers, ink-like rendering, margin notes. The entire app feels like a high-end paper notebook. Search and linking exist but are deliberately backgrounded.
